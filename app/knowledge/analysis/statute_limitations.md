# Statute of Limitations Determination Guide

## Purpose

This guide provides comprehensive methodology for determining whether debt claims are time-barred under statute of limitations laws, covering both general debts (诉讼时效) and judgment debts (执行时效).

## Part 1: General Statute of Limitations (诉讼时效)

### Overview

**Objective**: Determine if claim right can still be enforced through litigation.

**Key Determination Points**:
1. Start date (起算点)
2. Applicable period (2 years vs 3 years)
3. Interruption events (中断事由)
4. Suspension events (中止事由)
5. Final expiration date vs filing date

### Step 1: Determine Start Date

**Principle**: When creditor knew or should have known their right was infringed.

#### Contract Debts

**Start Date**: Day after contractual performance deadline expires

**Examples**:
- Contract states "payment due 2023-06-30" → Start date: 2023-07-01
- "Payment within 30 days of delivery" + delivered 2023-05-15 → Start date: 2023-06-15
- Multiple installments → Each installment has own start date

**Special Cases**:
- **No deadline specified**: When creditor can first claim performance
- **"Payment on demand"**: When creditor first makes demand

#### Tort Debts

**Start Date**: When creditor knew or should have known of damage and responsible party

**Note**: Less common in bankruptcy debt claims, usually contract-based.

#### Judgment/Legal Document Debts

**Start Date**: Day after judgment-specified performance deadline

**Special Note**: Judgment debts also subject to **execution statute** (see Part 2), which is usually more relevant.

#### Determination Methods

```
1. Review contract performance deadline clauses
2. If multiple deadlines, use last deadline for statute start
3. If no explicit deadline, apply legal rules or trade customs
4. Record determination basis clearly in analysis
```

### Step 2: Determine Applicable Period (2 Years vs 3 Years)

**Critical Transition**: Civil Code effective October 1, 2017 changed statute period from 2 years to 3 years.

#### Standard Process: "Old Law Expiration Date" Method

**Step-by-Step**:

1. **Calculate "old law expiration date"**: Start date + 2 years

2. **Apply transition rule**:
   - If "old law expiration date" < October 1, 2017 → Apply **2-year** period
   - If "old law expiration date" ≥ October 1, 2017 → Apply **3-year** period (from start date)

#### Examples

**Example 1 - 2-Year Period Applies**:
```
- Start date: June 1, 2015
- Old law expiration: June 1, 2015 + 2 years = June 1, 2017
- June 1, 2017 < October 1, 2017
- Result: Apply 2-year period
- Final expiration (without interruptions): June 1, 2017
```

**Example 2 - 3-Year Period Applies**:
```
- Start date: January 1, 2016
- Old law expiration: January 1, 2016 + 2 years = January 1, 2018
- January 1, 2018 ≥ October 1, 2017
- Result: Apply 3-year period (from January 1, 2016)
- Final expiration (without interruptions): January 1, 2019
```

**Example 3 - 3-Year Period (Recent Claim)**:
```
- Start date: March 15, 2022
- Old law expiration: March 15, 2024 (clearly after Oct 1, 2017)
- Result: Apply 3-year period
- Final expiration (without interruptions): March 15, 2025
```

### Step 3: Review Interruption Events (中断事由)

**Effect**: When valid interruption occurs, statute period RESTARTS from interruption date.

#### Type 1: Creditor Active Collection

##### 1.1 Written Demand

**Effective Evidence**:
- ✅ Demand letters (催款函)
- ✅ Attorney demand letters (律师函)
- ✅ Formal written notices

**Required Proof**:
- ✅ **Delivery proof**: Express delivery receipt, registered mail receipt, postal delivery certificate
- ✅ **Content specificity**: Letter clearly references the debt in question
- ✅ **Recipient verification**: Delivered to debtor or authorized representative

**Review Checklist**:
```
□ Written demand document exists and is authentic
□ Delivery proof is complete and valid
□ Recipient identity and authority verified
□ Content clearly relates to claimed debt
□ Date of delivery is within statute period
```

##### 1.2 Electronic Data Demand

**Effective Evidence**:
- ✅ WeChat chat records (微信聊天记录)
- ✅ Email exchanges
- ✅ SMS messages
- ✅ Other electronic communications

**Critical Requirements**:
- ✅ **Identity confirmation**: Must prove other party is debtor's legal representative, person in charge, or authorized representative
- ✅ **Content clarity**: Communication explicitly involves payment/performance of the debt
- ✅ **Evidence integrity**: Complete and unaltered records
- ✅ **Authentication**: Ability to authenticate sender/recipient identities

**Special Cautions**:
- WeChat records: Must confirm account holder's true identity
- Email: Must confirm email address ownership
- Must have evidence proving other party's authority to represent debtor

##### 1.3 Public Notice Collection

**Effective Conditions** (ALL must be met):
- ✅ **Prerequisite**: Can prove debtor's whereabouts are unknown (下落不明)
- ✅ **Media level**: Published in provincial-level or higher media
- ✅ **Content specificity**: Notice clearly identifies the specific debt
- ✅ **Procedural compliance**: Meets legal notice requirements

**Application**: Very limited - only when debtor cannot be located.

##### 1.4 Verbal/Recorded Demand

**Acceptable Evidence**:
- ✅ Audio recordings
- ✅ Video recordings
- ✅ Witness testimony

**Evidentiary Considerations**:
- ⚠️ **Lower probative value**: Generally weaker than written evidence
- ✅ **Supporting evidence needed**: Should form complete evidence chain
- ✅ **Authenticity verification**: Confirm recordings are genuine and complete

#### Type 2: Debtor Acknowledges Debt (Most Powerful!)

##### 2.1 Written Acknowledgment

**⚠️ MOST POWERFUL INTERRUPTION TYPE**

**Effective Documents**:
- ✅ Reconciliation statements (对账单) signed/stamped by debtor
- ✅ Debt confirmation letters (债权确认书)
- ✅ IOUs (欠条)
- ✅ Payment promise letters (还款承诺书)
- ✅ Signed acknowledgment on creditor's demand letter

**Requirements**:
- ✅ **Signature/Seal**: Debtor or authorized representative signs/stamps
- ✅ **Content clarity**: Clearly acknowledges the specific debt
- ✅ **Timeliness**: Made within statute period

**⚠️ MANDATORY TIME REVIEW**:

When debtor signs acknowledgment document:
1. **MUST record SPECIFIC DATE**: Not vague phrases like "before 2025 filing"
2. **MUST have EVIDENCE**: Document date or other time proof
3. **MUST RECALCULATE**: Statute period restarts from acknowledgment date
4. **MUST COMPARE**: Recalculated expiration date vs. bankruptcy filing date

**Example - Proper Analysis**:
```
Original statute:
- Start: 2020-06-01
- Period: 3 years
- Original expiration: 2023-06-01

Interruption:
- Debtor signed reconciliation statement: 2022-12-15
- Evidence: Statement dated and signed by debtor legal representative

Recalculation:
- New start: 2022-12-15
- Period: 3 years (continues to apply)
- New expiration: 2025-12-15

Comparison:
- Bankruptcy filing: 2025-05-12
- 2025-05-12 < 2025-12-15
- Conclusion: NOT time-barred
```

**Common Forms**:
- Debtor signs creditor's reconciliation statement
- Bilateral debt confirmation agreement
- Debtor unilaterally issues acknowledgment or IOU
- Debtor signs receipt on demand letter acknowledging debt

##### 2.2 Behavioral Acknowledgment (Partial Payment)

**Effective Actions**:
- ✅ **Partial payment**: Debtor pays part of the debt

**Critical Timing**:
- ✅ **MUST be before bankruptcy filing**: Payments after bankruptcy filing do NOT constitute acknowledgment
- ❌ **After bankruptcy filing**: Any payment/promise after bankruptcy accepted date is invalid for statute purposes

**Requirements**:
- ✅ **Payment proof**: Bank transfer records, receipts, etc.
- ✅ **Link to debt**: Evidence shows payment relates to claimed debt
- ✅ **Legal effect**: Constitutes acknowledgment of remaining debt, statute restarts

**⚠️ Important Exclusion**: Any debt acknowledgment or partial payment occurring ON OR AFTER bankruptcy acceptance date cannot interrupt statute.

#### Type 3: Judicial/Quasi-Judicial Actions

**Effective Actions**:
- ✅ **Lawsuit filing**: Creditor files lawsuit
- ✅ **Arbitration application**: Creditor applies for arbitration

**Review Points**:
- ✅ **Proof documents**: Complaint copy, arbitration application copy
- ✅ **Filing date**: Confirmed within statute period
- ✅ **Target**: Lawsuit/arbitration against the debtor for this debt

**Effect**: Statute interrupted from date of filing/application.

#### Interruption Analysis Documentation

**Standard Format**:

| 中断事由类型 | 具体情况 | 证据材料 | 中断日期 | 法律效果 |
|------------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| [类型] | [简要描述] | [证据编号、页码] | YYYY-MM-DD | 时效自此日重新起算 |

**Example**:
| 中断事由类型 | 具体情况 | 证据材料 | 中断日期 | 法律效果 |
|------------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| 债务人签字确认债务 | 债务人在对账单上签字盖章确认欠款132,216元 | 证据第15页对账单 | 2022-12-15 | 诉讼时效自2022年12月15日重新起算3年 |

### Step 4: Review Suspension Events (中止事由)

**Principle**: In last 6 months of statute period, if force majeure or other obstacles prevent claim, statute suspends.

#### Applicable Conditions

**Timing**: ONLY in **last 6 months** of statute period

**Qualifying Events**:
- **Force majeure**: Natural disasters, epidemic lockdowns, war, etc.
- **Other obstacles**:
  - Rights holder lacks capacity and has no legal representative
  - After inheritance starts but heir not determined
  - Other objective impediments preventing rights exercise

#### Suspension Period Calculation

**Calculation Rules**:
- **Suspension begins**: Date of suspension event occurrence
- **Suspension ends**: Date suspension cause is removed
- **New expiration**: From removal date + 6 months

**Example**:
```
Original expiration: 2025-03-01
Suspension event: COVID lockdown from 2024-12-01
Suspension removed: Lockdown lifted 2025-01-15
New expiration: 2025-01-15 + 6 months = 2025-07-15
```

#### Documentation Format

**If suspension applies**:
```
时效中止情况：
- 中止事由：[具体事由，如"新冠疫情封控"]
- 中止期间：自YYYY年MM月DD日至YYYY年MM月DD日
- 原届满日：YYYY年MM月DD日
- 新届满日：YYYY年MM月DD日（中止原因消除日 + 6个月）
```

**If no suspension**:
```
时效中止情况：期间未发生法定的时效中止事由
```

### Step 5: Comprehensive Judgment

#### Calculation Workflow

```
Step 1: Determine start date
         ↓
Step 2: Determine period (2y or 3y)
         ↓
Step 3: Calculate base expiration = Start + Period
         ↓
Step 4: Apply interruptions (if any)
        - Each interruption → Restart from interruption date
        - Use same period (2y or 3y as determined)
         ↓
Step 5: Apply suspensions (if any)
        - Extend from suspension removal date + 6 months
         ↓
Step 6: Final expiration date
         ↓
Step 7: Compare with bankruptcy filing date
```

#### Final Comparison

**NOT Time-Barred**:
- Final expiration date > Bankruptcy filing date
- **OR** Creditor filed claim before original expiration

**Time-Barred**:
- Final expiration date < Bankruptcy filing date

#### Conclusion Formats

**If NOT Time-Barred**:
```
诉讼时效判断结论：
- 适用时效期间：3年
- 时效起算日：2020年6月1日
- 中断情况：2022年12月15日，债务人签字确认债务，时效重新起算
- 时效最终届满日：2025年12月15日
- 债权申报日：2025年5月12日
- 结论：债权申报日早于时效届满日，**未超过诉讼时效**，债权可予以确认

说理：
本债权适用3年诉讼时效期间。虽自2020年6月1日起算，原应于2023年6月1日届满，但因2022年12月15日债务人签字确认债务，构成有效中断事由，诉讼时效自该日重新起算。最终届满日为2025年12月15日。债权人于2025年5月12日申报债权，早于时效届满日，故未超过诉讼时效。
```

**If Time-Barred**:
```
诉讼时效判断结论：
- 适用时效期间：3年
- 时效起算日：2018年6月1日
- 中断/中止情况：期间未发生有效的时效中断或中止事由
- 时效届满日：2021年6月1日
- 债权申报日：2025年5月12日
- 结论：债权申报日晚于时效届满日，**已超过诉讼时效**

处理建议：
因已超过诉讼时效，建议在"审查确认情况表"的"确认债权总额"栏填写【暂缓确认】。
```

**⚠️ Important**: Time-barred debts should be marked **【暂缓确认】** in review opinion table, not confirmed with specific amount.

### Reasoning Elements

**Complete analysis must include**:

1. **Period identification**: Clearly state 2-year or 3-year, cite legal basis
2. **Start date**: Brief explanation of start date and factual basis
3. **Interruption/Suspension**:
   - If exists: Briefly explain circumstances and effects
   - If not: State "期间未发生有效的时效中断或中止事由"
4. **Key dates**: List final expiration date and filing date clearly
5. **Conclusion**: Clear determination with reasoning

## Part 2: Execution Statute (执行时效)

### Applicable Scope

**ONLY for**: Debts based on effective legal documents (judgments, mediations, arbitration awards)

**Objective**: Determine if right to apply for execution has expired.

### Basic Rules

#### Statutory Period

- **Fixed period**: 2 years
- **Consequence**: If exceeded and debtor objects, court may rule not to execute

#### Start Date Determination

**Four Scenarios**:

##### Scenario 1: Specific Performance Deadline

**Rule**: From last day of performance period specified in legal document

**Example**:
```
Judgment: "Pay 100,000 yuan by June 30, 2023"
Execution statute starts: July 1, 2023
```

##### Scenario 2: Installment Performance

**Rule**: Each installment has separate execution statute starting from its due date

**Example**:
```
Mediation: "Pay 50,000 on March 31, 2023; 50,000 on June 30, 2023"
First installment statute starts: April 1, 2023
Second installment statute starts: July 1, 2023
```

##### Scenario 3: No Deadline Specified

**Rule**: From effective date of legal document

**Example**:
```
Judgment effective: May 15, 2023
No performance deadline mentioned
Execution statute starts: May 15, 2023
```

##### Scenario 4: Execution Settlement Broken

**Rule**: From last day of settlement agreement performance period

**Example**:
```
Original judgment: Effective 2022-06-01
Execution settlement: 2023-03-15, "pay by 2023-09-30"
Settlement broken (not paid)
New execution statute starts: October 1, 2023
```

### Execution Statute Interruption

**Valid Interruption Events**:

#### Event 1: Apply for Execution

**Actions**:
- Filing execution application with court
- Even if returned for incomplete materials, filing date counts if provable
- Applying to resume execution

**Effect**: Statute restarts from application date

#### Event 2: Demand from Obligee

**Requirements**:
- Clear demand for performance
- Evidence: Communication records, demand letters with delivery proof
- Unilateral claim without evidence not accepted

#### Event 3: Obligor Agrees to Perform

**Forms**:
- Explicit statement agreeing to perform
- Actual partial performance
- Must prove performance relates to this debt

#### Event 4: Other Interrupting Acts

**Examples**:
- Creditor applies for execution certificate
- Financial institution public notice collection
- Parties continue negotiating/reconciling on debt

**NOT Interruption**:
- ❌ Applying for retrial
- ❌ Applying to procuratorate for supervision (disputed)

### Comprehensive Judgment

#### Analysis Steps

```
1. Confirm debt nature: Is it based on effective legal document?
2. Calculate start date: Per performance deadline rules
3. Review interruptions: Check for valid interrupting events
4. Calculate final expiration: Start (or last interruption) + 2 years
5. Compare with filing date: Filing date vs. expiration date
```

#### Conclusion Handling

**NOT Exceeded**:
- Expiration date > Filing date
- **Processing**: Confirm debt normally

**Exceeded**:
- Expiration date < Filing date
- **Processing**: Mark **【不予确认】** in review opinion table
  - **Critical**: Execution period differs from statute of limitations
  - Per Supreme Court Bankruptcy Interpretation (II) Article 21 & Shanghai High Court Guidelines Article 9(2)
  - Exceeding execution period = NOT bankruptcy claim (different from statute-barred debts)

---

### ⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING: Common Misconception About Execution Period Expiration

**❌ WRONG UNDERSTANDING** (严重错误 - 必须避免):

> "执行时效届满并不消灭实体债权，债权人仍可在破产程序中申报"
>
> "Execution period expiration does not extinguish substantive debt rights. Creditors can still declare claims in bankruptcy proceedings."

**✅ CORRECT UNDERSTANDING** (正确理解):

Per **Supreme Court Interpretation on Bankruptcy Law (II) Article 21** and **Shanghai High Court Bankruptcy Work Guidelines Article 9(2)**:

**"超过诉讼时效或者申请执行期间的债权"不属于破产债权，应【不予确认】**

"Claims exceeding statute of limitations OR execution period are NOT bankruptcy claims and shall NOT be confirmed."

#### Key Distinction (关键区别)

| Aspect | Statute of Limitations (诉讼时效) | Execution Period (执行时效/申请执行期间) |
|--------|-----------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Legal Nature** | Right to win lawsuit extinguished | Right to apply for enforcement extinguished |
| **Effect on Substantive Right** | Debt still exists (natural obligation) | **In bankruptcy: Loses qualification as bankruptcy claim** |
| **Processing in Bankruptcy** | 【暂缓确认】Deferred confirmation | **【不予确认】Do NOT confirm** |
| **Reasoning** | Debtor can raise defense; uncertain | Creditor failed to exercise rights despite having legal instrument |
| **Legal Basis** | Civil Code Article 188 | Bankruptcy Law Interpretation (II) Article 21 + Court Guidelines |

#### Rationale (法理依据)

**Why Different Treatment?**

1. **Statute-Barred Debts**: Creditor never obtained judicial confirmation; substantive right debatable → Deferred confirmation pending further review

2. **Execution Period Expired**:
   - Creditor **already obtained effective legal instrument** (judgment/mediation/arbitration)
   - But **failed to apply for execution within statutory period** (generally 2 years)
   - Demonstrates **negligence in exercising rights**
   - **Bankruptcy proceedings should NOT provide remedy for such negligence**

**Policy Rationale**:
- Encourage creditors to timely exercise rights
- Prevent abuse of bankruptcy proceedings as "second chance" for negligent creditors
- Protect interests of diligent creditors in bankruptcy distribution

#### Processing Requirements

**When reviewing judgment-based claims**:

```
Step 1: Identify debt is based on effective legal document
Step 2: Calculate execution period (2 years from performance deadline)
Step 3: Check for execution application or interruption evidence
Step 4: If exceeded WITHOUT valid interruption:
        → Mark 【不予确认】(NOT 【暂缓确认】)
Step 5: Reasoning: "本债权已超过申请执行期间，债权人未能提供申请执行等中断事由证据。
                   根据《最高人民法院关于适用〈中华人民共和国企业破产法〉若干问题的规定（二）》
                   第二十一条及相关司法实践，不属于破产债权，建议【不予确认】。"
```

**⚠️ NEVER Use This Reasoning** (禁止使用以下表述):
- ❌ "执行时效届满不消灭实体债权"
- ❌ "债权人仍可依法申报并参与破产财产分配"
- ❌ "在破产程序中仍应按照债权的实体内容进行审查确认"

**✅ Correct Reasoning Template**:
```
本债权基于生效法律文书，但已超过申请执行期间。根据《最高人民法院关于适用〈中华人民共和国企业破产法〉
若干问题的规定（二）》第二十一条及《上海市高级人民法院破产审判工作规范指引（试行）》第九条第2款，
超过申请执行期间的债权不属于破产债权，建议【不予确认】。
```

## Part 3: Common Errors and Prevention

### Error 1: Insufficient Consideration of Interruptions

**❌ Problem**: Directly concluding time-barred without fully examining potential interruption events

**✅ Correct Approach**:
- Carefully review all evidence for possible interruptions
- When creditor mentions payments or collection efforts, investigate thoroughly
- For borderline cases, mark as "暂缓确认" with list of needed evidence

### Error 2: Vague Interruption Dates

**❌ Problem**: Recording interruption as "before 2025 filing" without specific date

**✅ Correct Approach**:
- **MUST record specific date** with evidence support
- **MUST recalculate** from interruption date
- **MUST compare** recalculated expiration with filing date

**Example**:
```
❌ Wrong: "债务人在申报前曾确认债务"
✅ Right: "债务人于2022年12月15日在对账单上签字确认债务（证据第15页）"
```

### Error 3: Missing Acceleration Clauses

**❌ Problem**: Not identifying contract acceleration clauses, leading to wrong start date

**✅ Correct Approach**:
- Search contracts for acceleration keywords: "加速到期", "提前到期", "立即到期"
- Review breach consequences carefully
- If acceleration triggered, start date may be earlier than normal deadline

### Error 4: Period Determination Errors

**❌ Problem**: Wrong application of 2-year vs 3-year period

**✅ Correct Approach**:
- **ALWAYS calculate "old law expiration date" (start + 2 years)**
- **ALWAYS compare** with October 1, 2017
- Follow transition rule precisely

### Error 5: Post-Bankruptcy Payments Counted

**❌ Problem**: Treating payments/promises after bankruptcy filing as interruption

**✅ Correct Approach**:
- **Exclude** all acknowledgments/payments on or after bankruptcy filing date
- Only pre-filing acknowledgments count for statute interruption

## Summary

### Key Principles

1. **General Statute**: 2-year or 3-year period based on "old law expiration" transition rule
2. **Execution Statute**: 2-year fixed period for judgment debts
3. **Most Powerful Interruption**: Debtor written acknowledgment (签字确认)
4. **Date Precision**: All interruption dates must be specific with evidence
5. **Recalculation**: Every interruption restarts the period
6. **Time-Barred Treatment**: Mark 【暂缓确认】, not specific confirmation amount

### Critical Reminders

- ⚠️ Debtor acknowledgments are strongest interruption - carefully review all signed documents
- ⚠️ MUST record specific dates for all interruptions
- ⚠️ MUST recalculate period from each interruption
- ⚠️ Post-bankruptcy payments/promises do NOT count
- ⚠️ Time-barred debts: Mark【暂缓确认】in review table
